S2 S2 Magic: Mixing Massive and Tiny Cells

To avoid massive write bloat for lines and polygons, Google Spanner allows inserting a mix of cell sizes. In this 8-bit example:

  • Level 1 cells cover large 2x2 areas. Their IDs are 32, 96, 160, 224.
  • Level 2 cells cover small 1x1 areas. Their IDs sit inside the numeric range of their parents.

We inserted 3 geometries: A massive Lake (represented by one Level 1 cell: 224), a Road (represented by three Level 2 cells: 8, 24, 40), and a Point (one Level 2 cell: 72).
